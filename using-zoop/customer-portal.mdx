The customer portal is a private web page you send to each customer. It requires no account or password — the link itself is the key. From the portal, customers can view their invoice history, pay outstanding invoices, save a card, and manage auto-pay. You control who has access.

## What customers can do

The portal shows the customer four main sections, in order:

- **Outstanding quotes** — any quotes you've sent that are still waiting on their response. The portal can't link directly to the accept page (quote links are one-time), so it tells the customer to check their original email.
- **Past invoices** — up to the 50 most recent invoices, newest first. Each row shows the invoice number, issue date, status, and total. Customers can tap any invoice to open and pay it.
- **Saved payment methods** — cards (and bank accounts, if any) on file. Customers can add a card, set a default, or remove a card. The portal shows which cards are linked to auto-pay plans.
- **Auto-pay** — active auto-pay arrangements tied to recurring plans. Customers can turn any of them off.

The bottom of the page shows your business phone and email (pulled from your [settings](/using-zoop/settings)) so customers can reach you if something looks wrong.

## What the invoice page shows

Each invoice opens its own public page. Customers see:

- Invoice number, issued date, and due date
- Line items — description, quantity, unit price, and total
- Subtotal, tax (itemized by rate name and percentage), and total
- A status banner — the exact message depends on status: paid, overdue, past due, payment didn't go through (dunning), or canceled (void)
- A pay block (see below)

Once paid, the invoice page shows a "Paid on [date]" banner with a link to the receipt. The receipt includes line items, totals, and the payment method label (e.g. "Visa •••• 4242") with the date and a Stripe charge ID.

### How payment works on the invoice page

The pay block adapts based on whether you have Stripe connected and whether you've set payment instructions:

| Stripe connected | Payment instructions set | What the customer sees |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Yes | No | "Pay now" button only |
| Yes | Yes | "Pay now" button + collapsible "Or pay offline" section |
| No | Yes | Instructions only — no online payment |
| No | No | A note to contact you directly |

When the customer pays online, they can optionally check:

- **Save card for future invoices** — stores the card on file so future invoices can be paid without re-entering card details.
- **Auto-pay future invoices for [plan]** — only shown on invoices linked to a recurring plan. Checking this also checks "save card" and locks it on.

## Generating a portal link

The portal link is generated from the customer detail page in Zoop. Only account owners can generate or revoke links — office staff can see whether a link is active but can't create one.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the customer">
    Go to [Customers](/using-zoop/customers) and open the customer's detail page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the portal access section">
    Scroll to the "Portal access" card. It shows whether an active link exists and when it was last used.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the link">
    Select **Generate portal link**. A dialog opens showing the full URL.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy and send it">
    Copy the link and send it to your customer — paste it into a text message, email, or your invoicing message. The link is shown only once. Close the dialog without copying and you'll need to rotate to a new link.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
The portal link is shown exactly once. Zoop stores only a one-way hash — if you close the dialog without copying, you cannot retrieve the same link. Generate a new one using **Rotate portal link**.
</Callout>

**Rotating the link** — selecting "Rotate portal link" on a customer who already has one replaces it with a new link. The old link stops working immediately. Use this if a link was forwarded somewhere it shouldn't have been.

**Revoking access** — selecting **Revoke** permanently disables the current link. The customer sees a 404 if they try to use it. You can generate a fresh link any time.

The "Last seen" timestamp on the portal access card updates each time the customer visits their portal page, so you can tell whether they've opened the link.

## Saved cards and auto-pay

When a customer saves a card through the portal or during invoice payment, it appears in the **Saved payment methods** section of their portal. They can:

- Set any card as the default (the radio button on each row)
- Add a new card using the **Add a card** button
- Remove a card — if that card backs an active auto-pay plan, the portal warns them that removing it will also turn off auto-pay on those plans

Each saved card shows brand, last 4 digits, expiry, and when it was added. Cards expiring within 30 days are flagged "Expiring soon."

### Turning off auto-pay

The **Auto-pay** section lists every active recurring auto-pay consent the customer has. Each row shows the plan name, the card it's charged to (brand and last 4), when the consent was granted, and any per-payment cap if one was set.

Customers can select **Turn off** next to any plan and confirm. After they confirm, Zoop stops charging that card automatically for future invoices under that plan — they'll receive the invoices as normal and pay manually instead.

If you set up auto-pay on behalf of a customer (zooper-attested consent), the row shows a "Zooper attested" badge. If the customer opted in themselves during checkout, it shows "Inline."

Customers can also turn off auto-pay using a direct link from the auto-pay confirmation email — the link opens the portal and pre-arms the confirmation dialog for that specific consent.

## What customers don't need

- An account or password — the link is the credential
- Your app — the portal is a plain web page that works in any browser
- You to be involved — customers can pay and manage their cards any time without contacting you
