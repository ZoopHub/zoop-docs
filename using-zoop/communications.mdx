Zoop lets you send SMS messages and emails to customers without leaving the app. Every message you send — and every reply you receive — lands in a single timeline on the customer's record, so nothing falls through the cracks.

## How messaging works

Zoop sends SMS through Twilio and email through Resend. You do not need accounts with either provider — Zoop handles the infrastructure. Outbound messages go from your business name and number (or email address) that Zoop has provisioned for your account.

Replies from customers come back into the same thread. Zoop matches an inbound message to a customer by the phone number or email address on their contact record.

## Sending an SMS

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a customer">
    Go to [Customers](/using-zoop/customers) and open the customer you want to contact.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the messages tab">
    Select the **Messages** tab on the customer page to see their communication timeline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compose your message">
    Select **Send SMS**, type your message, then send.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**SMS requires a phone number with SMS enabled.** If the customer's contact has no phone number on file, or if the number is marked as SMS-disabled, the send button will be disabled. Add or update the number from the [Customers](/using-zoop/customers) page first.

## The message timeline

Every message tied to a customer — sent by you, received from them, or triggered automatically by Zoop — appears in the timeline in chronological order. Each entry shows:

- Channel (SMS or email)
- Direction (sent or received)
- Date and time
- A preview of the message body
- Delivery status

Statuses for outbound messages move through: **queued → sending → delivered**. If delivery fails, the status shows **failed** with a reason. Inbound messages arrive as **received**.

## Receiving replies

When a customer replies to an SMS or email, Zoop matches the incoming message back to their record using their phone number or email address. The reply appears in the timeline automatically.

**What happens when a message can't be matched?** If the sender's number or email is not on any customer contact, Zoop holds the message in a quarantine queue so it is not lost.

## Automated messages

Zoop sends some messages on your behalf automatically, without you having to compose anything.

### Invoice notifications

When you send an invoice to a customer, Zoop can deliver it by SMS, email, or both. The message includes the invoice total and — if you have a payment link available — a direct link for online payment. If you have offline payment instructions set up in your account settings, those are included too.

### Payment receipts

After a payment is recorded on an invoice, Zoop automatically sends a receipt to the customer. The email receipt includes a line-item breakdown and a link to view the receipt online. The SMS receipt is a short confirmation with the amount paid and a link.

These automated receipts appear in the customer's timeline just like manual messages.

## Linking a message to a job, invoice, or quote

Messages can be associated with a specific job, invoice, or quote. When a message is linked, it appears in the timeline for that record as well as the customer's general timeline. Automated messages — like invoice notifications — are linked automatically. Manual messages can be linked at the time you send them.

## Contacting a specific contact

If a customer has more than one contact on file (for example, a homeowner and their property manager), you can choose which contact receives the message. The default is the primary contact.

## Things to know

- **SMS length.** Zoop enforces a 1,600-character limit on SMS messages. Keep messages concise — a standard SMS segment is 160 characters, and longer messages are split by carriers.
- **Opted-out contacts.** If a contact has opted out of SMS, Zoop will not send to that number. The timeline will reflect this rather than silently skipping.
- **No double sends.** Zoop deduplicates sends using an internal key, so retrying a failed action will not result in the same message going out twice.
- **Delivery confirmation.** Twilio reports delivery status back to Zoop. The timeline updates automatically when a message is confirmed delivered or fails.
