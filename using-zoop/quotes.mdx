Quotes let you price out work, share a link with the customer for their approval, and convert directly to a job or invoice once they accept.

## Quote statuses

| Status | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| Draft | Not yet sent. You can still edit everything. |
| Sent | Shared with the customer. Awaiting their response. |
| Accepted | Customer approved it. Ready to convert. |
| Declined | Customer declined. The quote is preserved for your records. |

Archiving a quote removes it from the default list without deleting it permanently.

## Create a quote

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Quotes and select New quote">
    From the sidebar, go to **Quotes** and select **New quote** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a title">
    Give the quote a descriptive title — for example, "742 Evergreen — spring cleanup". You can leave the title as "Untitled quote" and rename it later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach a customer (optional)">
    Use the customer search to link the quote to an existing customer. You can also skip this and assign the customer later from the quote editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Create quote">
    Zoop creates the quote in **draft** status and opens the editor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Add line items

Inside the quote editor, the line items table is where you price the work.

- Select **Add line** to insert a row.
- Type a description, then set the quantity and unit price.
- To pull in items from your pricebook, start typing in the description field and select a matching catalog item — the price fills in automatically.
- Repeat for each service or material.

## Organize with sections (optional)

Sections let you group line items under named phases or areas — for example "Front yard" and "Back yard", or "Phase 1" and "Phase 2".

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a section">
    In the **Sections** area of the editor, select **Add section**. Zoop creates a card labelled "Section 1".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rename the section">
    Select the section label to edit it inline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set dates (optional)">
    Select **+ Add dates** to attach a start and end date to the section. These appear as a badge on the section card and are useful for multi-phase proposals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add line items inside the section">
    Each section card has its own line items table. Line items added there are pinned to that section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reorder sections">
    Use the up and down arrows in the section header to change the order. Deleting a section moves its line items back to the quote level — they are not lost.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Attach files

You can attach images, PDFs, blueprints, and videos to a quote — either at the quote level or inside a specific section.

- Select **Add attachment** in the **Attachments** panel.
- Images open in a lightbox when selected. PDFs, blueprints, and videos open in a new tab.
- To remove a file, hover the thumbnail and select the trash icon.

## Use the lawn estimator

For landscaping and lawn care work, you can draw a polygon on a satellite map to capture the yard's square footage and attach it to the quote as a measurement.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lawn estimator">
    In the **Tool inputs** panel inside the quote editor, select **Add measurement**, then choose **Lawn measurement**.

    Alternatively, from the Quotes list page select **New lawn measurement** to start the estimator without an existing quote.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the property">
    The map opens in satellite view. Navigate to the property address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Draw the lawn area">
    Click to place each corner of the polygon around the area you want to measure. Click the first point again — or double-click — to close the shape.

    While placing points, press **Backspace** (when your cursor is inside the map) to undo the last point.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the area readout">
    Once the polygon is closed, Zoop calculates the area in square feet and displays it. Select **Edit** to adjust the shape by dragging its vertices, or **Clear** to start over.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the measurement">
    Save the measurement to attach it to the quote. It appears as a chip in the **Tool inputs** panel labelled with the area and address — for example "2,340 sq ft — 742 Evergreen Terrace".
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Share with a customer

Once the quote is ready, send the customer a link to review and accept it. No account is required on their end.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Share with customer">
    In the quote editor header, select **Share with customer**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set an expiry (optional)">
    Choose a date and time for the link to expire. Leave blank for a link that never expires.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Issue new link">
    Select **Issue new link**. Zoop generates a secure share URL and shows it once — copy it immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the link">
    Paste the link into a text or email to your customer. They can review the quote and accept or decline without logging in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
The share URL is shown only once after you issue it. If you close the dialog without copying it, you will need to issue a new link — which invalidates the previous one.
</Callout>

### Revoke a share link

To stop a link from working, open **Share with customer** and select **Revoke**. The customer's link becomes invalid immediately.

## Download the PDF

From the quote editor header, select **Download PDF** to open a letter-size proposal PDF in a new tab.

The PDF leads with your business name and a "Proposal" label, then the quote title, status, and issue date (the date the quote was created). Each section appears as its own card — with its label, description, and date range when set — followed by a line-items table with description, quantity, unit price, and total. Line items not assigned to a section appear under an **Other** heading. Attachments are listed by filename, not embedded as images. A totals card shows the subtotal and total, and the footer carries your business phone and email (from your company settings) above a "Powered by Zoop" line.

## Convert to a job

Once a quote is accepted, you can convert it to a job.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the accepted quote">
    The sidebar shows **What would you like to do next?** when the quote status is **Accepted**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Create job">
    Select **Create job**. A confirmation dialog opens — the quote stays on file as-is.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Select **Create job** in the dialog. Zoop creates the job using the quote's title and opens it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">TODO(screenshot): "What would you like to do next?" sidebar panel on an accepted quote, with the Create job and Create invoice buttons.</Callout>

You can also convert a quote from the Quotes list — open the row actions menu (the three-dot icon) and select **Convert to job**. The row-actions dialog lets you optionally override the job title, assign team members, and choose a schedule (one-time or recurring).

See [Jobs](/using-zoop/jobs) for what to do after a job is created.

## Convert to an invoice

To bill the customer directly without scheduling a job first:

1. Open the accepted quote.
2. In the sidebar, select **Create invoice**.
3. Zoop creates an invoice pre-filled with the quote's line items and opens it.

<Callout type="warning">
Both **Create job** and **Create invoice** require at least one line item on the quote. Add line items first if the buttons are disabled.
</Callout>

See [Invoices](/using-zoop/invoices) for how to send the invoice and record payment.

---

## Archive a quote

Archiving hides a quote from the list without deleting it — its history is preserved.

There is no archive control in the Quotes UI today. The Quotes list row actions menu offers **Open quote**, **Open in lawn editor** (for lawn quotes), and **Convert to job** — no archive option. The quote editor has no archive button either. Archiving is currently only available through the API, where it is restricted to the account owner.

Once a quote is archived, it drops off every tab — **All**, **Draft**, **Sent**, **Accepted**, and **Declined**. There is no **Archived** tab or status filter in the Quotes list, so an archived quote is no longer visible in Zoop.
