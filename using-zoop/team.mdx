This page covers how to invite people to your Zoop account, control what they can do, and group them into crews for easy job assignment.

Team management lives under **Team** in the sidebar. From here you invite teammates, change their roles, and organize them into crews.

Only owners can access the Team and Crews pages. Office and tech members see a "no access" notice if they navigate there directly.

<Image src="https://doc-assets.zoop.pro/images/using-zoop/team/team-settings-overview.png" alt="Team settings page showing the members table with one owner and the invite a new member form with role defaulting to Tech" />

## Roles

A role controls what a person can see and do in Zoop. Every person you invite gets exactly one role.

| Role | What they can do |
|------|-----------------|
| **Owner** | Full access — billing, integrations, team management, and everything below. |
| **Office** | Operational access — schedule, customers, jobs. No financials, no settings. |
| **Tech** | Own assigned jobs only. No customer list, no settings. |

The invite form defaults to **Tech** — the most limited role. Change the role before sending if the person needs broader access.

<Callout type="warning">
Every Zoop account must have at least one owner at all times. Zoop prevents you from demoting or removing the last remaining owner.
</Callout>

## Invite a teammate

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Team settings">
    Select **Team** in the sidebar. Scroll to the **Invite a new member** section at the bottom of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter their email">
    Type the person's email address. If they already have a Zoop account, you'll see a note: "Existing Zoop user — they'll skip account creation."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a role">
    Pick **Owner**, **Office**, or **Tech**. A short description under the dropdown explains what each role can do.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invite">
    Select **Send invite**. Zoop emails them a link to accept and set up their account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The invite appears in the **Pending invites** section above the member list until the person accepts.

---

## Manage pending invites

Pending invites show the recipient's email, the role they were invited as, who sent the invite, when it was sent, and when it expires.

From the pending-invites table you can:

- **Resend** — send the invite email again (useful if it landed in spam or the link expired). The expiry resets when you resend.
- **Revoke** — cancel the invite. The link in the email stops working immediately.

<Image src="https://doc-assets.zoop.pro/images/using-zoop/team/pending-invites.png" alt="The Team settings page with a Pending invites table — the invited email, role, who invited them, when, expiry, and resend and revoke icons." />

## Change a member's role

Find the person in the **Members** table and use the role dropdown in their row. The change takes effect immediately — no confirmation step. If something goes wrong, Zoop shows an error message and restores the previous role in the table.

Lowering someone's role also automatically tightens their MCP & API access ceiling down to the new role's defaults, so a demotion never leaves them with more outside access than their new role allows.

You cannot change your own role if you are the only owner of the account.

---

## Remove a member

Select the remove icon (the circle-with-slash) on the member's row and confirm. The person loses access immediately. Their past work stays in Zoop — removing them only stops their access. You can invite them back at any time.

You cannot remove the last owner of the account.

---

## Control MCP & API access per member

Roles set what a person sees in Zoop. This setting controls something separate: what they — and any API key or AI assistant they connect — can reach through Zoop's MCP server and API. Think of it as a ceiling.

On the **Members** table, each row has a shield icon ("Manage MCP & API access for {name}"). Select it to open the **MCP & API access** dialog. It reads "Choose what {name} can reach through Zoop's MCP server and API. This is the ceiling for any key or assistant they connect." The dialog starts "Seeded from this member's current access," and a **Reset to {role} defaults** button puts it back to the defaults for their role.

<Image src="https://doc-assets.zoop.pro/images/using-zoop/team/team-list.png" alt="The team Members table, each row showing the member's role, last active, and the shield icon used to manage their MCP & API access." />

You set access one area at a time. Each of these can be **None**, **View**, or **Edit**:

- Customers & contacts
- Jobs & scheduling
- Quotes & estimates
- Invoices & payments
- Catalog & pricing
- Messages & notes
- Business settings

Owners also see an **Admin access** toggle — billing, tenant settings, and credentials.

Select **Save access** when done.

<Image src="https://doc-assets.zoop.pro/images/using-zoop/team/mcp-api-access-dialog.png" alt="The MCP & API access dialog with the seven permission areas set to None, View, or Edit, plus an owner-only Admin access toggle." />

<Callout type="warning">
This is the ceiling — the most any API key or AI assistant that member connects can ever do. Tightening an area immediately limits everything they've already connected, not just future connections.
</Callout>

### Set access when you invite someone

The invite form has a collapsible **Customize MCP/API access** preview that shows the selected role's defaults. The ceiling is applied from the role when the member accepts. Each role's defaults:

| Role | Default access |
|------|----------------|
| **Owner** | Full access including billing, integrations, and team management. |
| **Office** | Operational access — schedule, customers, jobs. No financials, no settings. |
| **Tech** | Own assigned jobs only. No customer list, no settings. |

New members default to **Tech**. After they accept, you can fine-tune their ceiling any time with the shield icon above.

To see what each member's apps are actually doing with that access, owners can review the [Connected apps list](/using-zoop/settings#connected-apps) in Settings. For more on how Zoop's AI features use this access, see [Your AI assistant](/ai/assistant).

## Crews

A crew is a named group of teammates. When you dispatch a job to a crew, all members of that crew are assigned to it — no need to add people one by one.

Crews are managed by selecting **Team** in the sidebar, then **Crews** from the submenu.

<Image src="https://doc-assets.zoop.pro/images/using-zoop/team/crews-table.png" alt="The Crews page showing a crew with its member count and row actions" />

### Create a crew

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Crews">
    Select **Team** in the sidebar, then **Crews** from the submenu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select New crew">
    Select the **New crew** button in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the crew">
    Give it a short name your team will recognise — for example, "Tuesday crew" or "Henderson & Mike". Select **Create**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Add or remove crew members

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open manage members">
    On the crew's row, select the people icon. A dialog opens showing current members and available teammates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a teammate">
    In the **Available teammates** section, select the plus icon next to the person's name. They appear in the **In this crew** list immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove a teammate">
    In the **In this crew** section, select the minus icon next to the person's name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the dialog">
    Select **Done** when finished. Changes are saved as you go — there is no separate save step.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Rename a crew

On the crew's row, select the pencil icon, edit the name, and select **Save**. The new name appears on any future jobs. Jobs that ran before the rename keep the old name on their history.

### Archive a crew

Archiving is safe — it hides a crew from the dispatch picker without deleting any history. Select the circle-with-slash icon on the crew's row and confirm. Jobs that already used the crew keep the crew name in their history.

To see archived crews, turn on the **Show archived** toggle above the table.

### Unarchive a crew

With **Show archived** enabled, find the crew in the table and select the undo icon. The crew becomes available in the dispatch picker again.

---

## Dispatch a crew to a job

Crew assignment happens from the [dispatch board](/using-zoop/dispatch) or from the job detail page, not from Settings. Once you have created a crew and added members, the crew name appears in the assignee picker when you schedule or edit a job.
