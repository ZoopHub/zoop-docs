Zoop has a small set of AI-powered automations that run in the background so you can stay on the job. You turn them on in [Settings](/using-zoop/settings) under the **AI & automation** tab.

## AI virtual receptionist

When you are on a job and cannot answer your phone, the AI receptionist can reply automatically to customers who text or call your Zoop business number. It introduces itself and answers common questions based on what you tell it.

Receptionist conversations are designed to land on the customer's timeline. Open a customer and filter the timeline to **AI sessions** to see them alongside the customer's other SMS, email, and call activity.

### What the receptionist can say

You control what information it shares. In the **AI knowledge base** section you can set:

- **Dispatch fee** — if you charge one, the receptionist will quote it when a customer asks.
- **Services offered** — a list of what you do (for example: water heater replacement, drain cleaning).
- **Services not offered** — a list of what you do not do (for example: commercial jobs, septic work).
- **Service area description** — a plain-English description of your coverage (for example: Greater Orlando area within 25 miles of zip 32801).

The receptionist will only answer within those guardrails. It will not invent prices, offer discounts, or make commitments beyond what you have specified.

### Intro message

The **intro message** is the first thing the receptionist sends when it takes over. You write it. Keep it short and personal — something like: "Hi, I'm currently on a job and can't get to my phone right now. I'll follow up as soon as I'm done."

There is a 500-character limit. The settings form shows an SMS preview of your message as you type it.

### Turn it on

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open settings">
    Go to [Settings](/using-zoop/settings) and select the **AI & automation** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the receptionist">
    Toggle **Enable AI receptionist** on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your intro message">
    Type your intro message in the **Intro message** field. The SMS preview updates as you type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the knowledge base">
    Add your dispatch fee, services offered, services not offered, and service area description. These fields appear once the toggle is on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Select **Save changes**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
The AI receptionist only activates on your Zoop business number. If customers reach you on a personal cell number, this feature has no effect.
</Callout>

## Maintenance reminders

When this is on, Zoop prompts you to schedule a follow-up maintenance visit after equipment installations — for example, after you install an A/C unit or a water heater. The reminder surfaces inside Zoop; it does not automatically contact the customer.

To turn it on, go to [Settings](/using-zoop/settings) → **AI & automation** and toggle **Enable Maintenance Reminders** on.

## Auto-text when a job is running late

Zoop watches your route in real time. When a hard-window visit's estimated arrival slips past the promised window, Zoop can automatically send the customer a heads-up text.

This is **off by default.** When it is off, you or your tech send the message manually from the route cockpit. Turn it on only if you want Zoop to send it without you reviewing it first.

To enable it, go to [Settings](/using-zoop/settings) → **Notifications** and toggle **Auto-text customers when a job is likely to run late**.

<Callout type="info">
This only fires for hard-window visits (where you confirmed a specific arrival window with the customer). Flexible or FYI-window visits are not affected.
</Callout>

## Missed jobs

If a job's scheduled day passes without it being marked done, it appears in a **Needs rescheduling** tray at the top of the [dispatch board](/using-zoop/dispatch). From there you can generate a reschedule link and send it to the customer so they can pick a new time.

Zoop does not automatically move or reassign the job. You decide whether and when to send the link.

## Job drafting from notes (MCP / AI assistant)

If you use an external AI assistant — such as Claude Desktop — connected to Zoop via MCP, you can dictate rough notes and have the assistant draft a job for you. You review and confirm before anything is saved.

This works through Zoop's **customer-job-draft** prompt. You provide a customer and your spoken or typed notes; the assistant proposes a job title and description in plain language, then calls `jobs.create` once you confirm.

There is no AI chat inside Zoop itself for this. Job drafting from notes is available only through an external MCP client such as Claude Desktop — it is a developer and power-user feature. See [MCP](/developers/mcp) for setup instructions.

## Voice notes

In the **Zoop mobile app**, you can record a voice note and Zoop transcribes it to text for you. Tap the microphone in the note composer, talk through what happened on the job, and Zoop turns it into a note you can edit and save. The original audio is kept as an attachment on the note.

Voice transcription has a monthly limit per business. When you reach it, transcription pauses until the limit resets on the 1st of the month.

In the web app, the note composer shows a microphone button too, but it is disabled — its tooltip reads "Voice notes coming soon." Use the mobile app to capture voice notes for now.
