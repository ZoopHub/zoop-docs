A **job series** is a template that Zoop uses to generate individual jobs on a schedule. You set the title, pricing, assigned techs, and recurrence rule once. Zoop then creates each visit automatically and keeps them on your [calendar](/using-zoop/calendar).

## Create a recurring job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the new job form">
    Go to [Jobs](/using-zoop/jobs) and tap **New job**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the basics">
    Add a title, customer, and any assigned techs. Optionally add line items or a flat price — these become the default for every generated visit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch to recurring">
    Below the assignment section, tap **Recurring** in the schedule toggle. The one-time date fields are replaced by the recurrence editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the schedule">
    Pick a frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly) and an interval. For weekly rules, select which days of the week. For monthly rules, choose between same date, a specific weekday position (e.g. second Tuesday), or the last weekday of the month.

    Then set:
    - **Start date** — the date the first visit falls on.
    - **Timezone** — used to anchor the schedule to local time so visits don't drift with daylight saving.
    - **End** — choose **Never**, **On date**, or **After N occurrences**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Tap **Create job**. Zoop saves the series and immediately creates the first 8 weeks of visits. You land on the series detail page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Image src="/images/using-zoop/recurring-jobs/recurrence-editor.png" alt="New job form with Recurring selected, showing frequency set to weekly, day-of-week picker, start date, timezone, and end controls" />

## How Zoop generates visits

When you create or update a series, Zoop materializes an 8-week rolling window of individual jobs. Each generated job inherits the series title, pricing, line items, and assignments. A daily cron catches up any missed generation, so your upcoming list stays current even if a background step ran slowly.

Generated jobs start in **scheduled** status. You can open any individual visit and edit it independently without affecting the rest of the series.

## View a series

From the [Jobs](/using-zoop/jobs) list, tap a job that has the recurring icon. On the detail page, the schedule section shows the recurrence rule and a "View series" link. From the series detail page you can see:

- The recurrence summary (e.g. "Repeats every week on Monday, Thursday").
- The customer linked to the series.
- Pricing — either a flat price or itemized line items.
- **Upcoming** — visits that haven't happened yet.
- **Past** — visits that are done or were cancelled. These are permanent records and are never removed.

## Edit a series

If you need to change the title, schedule, pricing, or assignments for all future visits, open any upcoming visit and tap **Edit**. You'll be asked how far the change should reach:

- **Just this occurrence** — changes only this single visit. The rest of the series is unaffected.
- **This and future occurrences** — changes this visit and all visits from that point forward. Completed or cancelled visits before this date are left alone.
- **All occurrences** — changes the series template. Zoop deletes future unscheduled and scheduled visits, then regenerates them from the updated template. Done or cancelled visits are preserved as historical records.

<Callout type="warning">
Editing with "all occurrences" replaces every upcoming visit. If you've manually adjusted the time or details of individual visits that haven't happened yet, those changes will be overwritten.
</Callout>

To edit the series template directly, go to the series detail page and tap **Edit template**. This is equivalent to choosing "all occurrences" from an upcoming visit. The button only appears when at least one upcoming visit exists.

### What changes and what doesn't

| What you change | Just this occurrence | This and future | All occurrences |
|---|---|---|---|
| Title, notes, pricing | This visit only | This visit onward | All upcoming visits |
| Recurrence schedule | No effect | New schedule from here | New schedule from start |
| Assigned techs | This visit only | This visit onward | All upcoming visits |
| Done or cancelled visits | Untouched | Untouched | Untouched |

## End a series

To stop new visits from being created, go to the series detail page and tap **Delete series**. Zoop caps the series end date to today, cancels every upcoming unscheduled or scheduled visit, and keeps all past completed jobs as historical records.

<Callout type="warning">
Ending a series cancels all upcoming visits immediately. This cannot be undone from the UI. If you only want to pause work temporarily, consider editing individual upcoming visits to a cancelled status instead.
</Callout>

## Recurring jobs from a quote

You can also create a recurring job series from an accepted quote. On the quote detail page, when converting to a job, choose **Recurring** and configure the schedule. Zoop links the series back to the originating quote for reference. See [Quotes](/using-zoop/quotes) for details on the conversion flow.
