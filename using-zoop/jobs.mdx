A job is the unit of work in Zoop. Every service call — whether it's a one-time visit or part of a recurring series — lives here. You can create a job from scratch or convert an accepted quote, assign techs or a crew, add line items, attach photos and notes, and mark it done when the work is finished.

<Image src="https://doc-assets.zoop.pro/images/using-zoop/jobs/jobs-list-tabs.png" alt="Jobs list showing the All, Unscheduled, Scheduled, and Done tabs with a mix of scheduled and done jobs" />

## Job statuses

Every job has one of four statuses:

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| Unscheduled | Created but no start time set yet. |
| Scheduled | A start time has been set. |
| Done | Work is complete. Tapping "Mark done" moves the job here. |
| Cancelled | Job was called off. |

The jobs list has tabs for All, Unscheduled, Scheduled, and Done. You can also search by job title.

## Create a job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the jobs list">
    Go to **Jobs** in the sidebar and tap **New job**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the basics">
    - **Title** — required. Keep it specific: "Replace water heater" or "Spring lawn treatment" is more useful than "Job 14".
    - **Customer** — search and select the customer. You can leave this blank and add it later.
    - **Assigned techs** — search by name and select one or more team members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the schedule">
    Choose whether this is a one-time job or a recurring job.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="One-time">
        Set a **Start** and, optionally, an **End** time. If you leave both blank, the job is created as unscheduled — you can add the time later from the job detail page or from the [calendar](/using-zoop/calendar).
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Recurring">
        Configure the recurrence pattern (daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly), the start date, the timezone, and when the series ends. Zoop creates the series template and materializes the upcoming occurrences. See [recurring jobs](/using-zoop/recurring-jobs) for full details.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add line items">
    Add what you're charging for. Each line item has a description, quantity, and unit price. As you type, Zoop searches your [pricebook](/using-zoop/pricebook) and suggests matching items — select one to pre-fill the description and price.

    If you have a single agreed price for the whole job, enter it in the **Flat price** field. When a flat price is set, the line-item subtotal is for reference only.

    On any manual line item (one you typed rather than picked from the pricebook), you'll see a "Save to pricebook" link. Tap it to add that item to your catalog so you can reuse it in future jobs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add notes">
    The **Notes** field is for job-level context: access instructions, gate codes, what the customer told you, what to watch out for. This is separate from the structured notes panel on the job detail page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Tap **Create job**. Zoop opens the job detail page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create a job from a quote

When a quote is accepted, you can convert it to a job directly from the quote detail page. The job inherits the line items and customer from the quote. A "Source quote" link appears in the job's connections panel so you can navigate back.

See [quotes](/using-zoop/quotes) for how to send and accept quotes.

## Job detail page

The job detail page is where you manage everything about a single job.

<Image src="https://doc-assets.zoop.pro/images/using-zoop/jobs/job-detail-overview.png" alt="Job detail page showing the status badge, Edit and Mark done buttons, Customer, Schedule, and Assigned techs cards, line-items table, and Photos and Notes sections" />

### Overview cards

Three cards appear near the top of the page:

- **Customer** — name, phone, and email. Tap the name to open the customer record.
- **Schedule** — the start time, end time, and duration for a one-time job. For a recurring instance, this shows the recurrence pattern and a link to the full series.
- **Assigned techs** — the team members currently assigned to this job.

### Line items

The line-items table shows each item with its quantity, unit price, and row total, plus a subtotal at the bottom. If a flat price is set, it appears instead and the table is shown for reference only.

### Photos and videos

Tap **Upload** in the "Photos and videos" section to attach a photo or video from your device. Uploaded media appears in a grid. Tap any thumbnail to open it full-screen; use the left and right arrows (or arrow keys) to navigate between files.

### Notes

The notes panel below the main card holds structured notes for this job — things added by you or a teammate with a timestamp and author. Tap **Add note** in the page header to write one.

The job description field (set when creating or editing the job) is separate from these structured notes and appears above the line-items section when it has content.

### Connections

If the job came from a quote, a "Connections" sidebar shows the source quote and its status. Any invoices linked to this job also appear here.

## Edit a job

Tap **Edit** on the job detail page. You can change the title, customer, assigned techs, schedule, line items, and notes.

For a recurring job, Zoop asks which occurrences to update:

| Option | What changes |
|---|---|
| Just this occurrence | Only this date — others are unchanged. |
| This and future occurrences | Splits the series from this date forward. |
| All occurrences | Updates the series template for every occurrence. Past completed jobs are preserved. |

## Assign a crew

When editing a one-time job, the "Assigned techs" picker also shows your saved crews. Selecting a crew adds all of its current members to the job. If the crew has no members, Zoop tells you and skips the assignment.

See [team](/using-zoop/team) for how to create and manage crews.

## Mark a job done

Tap **Mark done** on the job detail page. The status changes to done and the button disappears. You can still edit the job after it is done.

## Dispatch and scheduling

The [dispatch](/using-zoop/dispatch) view shows your jobs on a map and a schedule board so you can route efficiently and assign work to your team. The [calendar](/using-zoop/calendar) gives a time-based view of all scheduled jobs.

## Recurring jobs

Recurring jobs run on a schedule — weekly maintenance, monthly inspections, and so on. They are managed through a series template that generates individual job occurrences. See [recurring jobs](/using-zoop/recurring-jobs) for a full guide.
