The dashboard is the first thing you see when you open Zoop. It pulls live numbers from across your account so you can spot problems — a customer whose card is about to expire, an invoice nobody has paid in weeks — without digging through every screen.

<Image src="/images/using-zoop/dashboard/dashboard-full.png" alt="Zoop dashboard showing the four business overview stat cards, expiring cards card, route intelligence card, and payment recovery card" />

## What's on the dashboard

### Business overview

Four summary numbers sit at the top:

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| Open jobs | Jobs that are not yet complete. |
| Upcoming schedule | Jobs scheduled for this week. |
| Pending invoices | Total dollar value of invoices awaiting payment. |
| Active customers | Customers currently on your books. |

### Expiring cards

This card shows how many customers have a saved card that is expiring within the next 30 days and has not been automatically refreshed by the card network.

Most cards are refreshed silently — Zoop never shows those. The ones that appear here genuinely need your attention before a recurring charge fails.

- If only one customer is affected, the card links straight to that customer's page.
- If more than one customer is affected, the link takes you to a filtered view of your [customers](/using-zoop/customers) list.
- When everything is current, the card reads "All up to date."

### Route intelligence

This card is a shortcut to your route intelligence view, which shows capacity, travel time, and clustering for your team's week. Select the card to open it.

### Payment recovery

The payment recovery card (labeled "Payment recovery") tracks invoices that are overdue or in a failed-payment cycle. It breaks them into three buckets:

| Bucket | What it means |
|---|---|
| Stripe retrying | Stripe is automatically retrying a failed payment on its schedule. |
| Zoop nudging | The invoice is in sent status and Zoop is sending follow-up reminders. |
| Uncollectible — review needed | Invoices marked uncollectible in the last 30 days. These need a decision: write them off, or re-open and try again. |

Each non-empty row links to the [invoices](/using-zoop/invoices) list filtered to that bucket, so you can act on the right ones without hunting.

When there is nothing in any bucket, the card reads "All caught up."

## Getting started section

If your account is new, the bottom of the dashboard shows two quick actions: **Add a customer** and **Create a job**. These are the two steps that unlock everything else in Zoop.
