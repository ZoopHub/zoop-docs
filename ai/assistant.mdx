Zoop is the back-office assistant you always wished you had — and it lives in the chat app already on your phone. Connect Zoop to **ChatGPT**, **Claude**, **Perplexity**, or any app that speaks MCP, and you run your business by texting: ask what's on your schedule, book a job, pull up a customer's address, draft a quote or an invoice — all in plain language, from the truck.

You're just a conversation away. Call Zoop anytime.

<Callout kind="note" collapsed="false">
  This works inside the AI app you already use. There's nothing new to learn and no extra app to open — you talk to Zoop the same way you'd text your office manager.
</Callout>

## What you can ask it to do

Talk to Zoop like a person. A few of the things it handles:

- **Look things up** — "What's Maxine's phone number?" · "What's the gate code at the Carter place?" · "What did I quote the Reyes job?"

- **Your day** — "What's on my schedule today?" · "What's coming up this week?" · "Anything that needs my attention?"

- **Book and move work** — "Book Edward Stone for a cabinet refinish Thursday at 9." · "Reschedule the pressure-washing job to Friday." · "Mark the Carter job done."

- **Customers** — "Add a new customer: Dana Lee, 555-0188." · "Add their shop as a second service address."

- **Quotes and invoices** — "Start a quote for the bathroom remodel." · "Draft an invoice for today's work — two hours of labor at $95.00 plus a $45.00 part."

- **Notes** — "Leave a note on the Henderson job: bring the half-inch fittings."

- **Pricebook, recurring work, and taxes** — "What do I charge for a water-heater install?" · "Set up weekly mowing for the Ortiz account every Monday."

After it does something, it confirms in a sentence and hands you a **Verify on Zoop** link straight to the record — tap it to open and double-check.

## It talks like your office manager

Short, plain, and human. Zoop speaks in names, dates, and dollars — never codes or screens. It reads your customers' contact details back to you without fuss (they're your records). And before anything it can't undo — voiding an invoice, cancelling a job — it names it and asks first.

## Send it a photo

Your AI app can see what you see. Snap a photo of a handwritten work order, a part's label, a whiteboard full of measurements, or another company's quote, and ask Zoop to act on it — your assistant reads the details and drafts the quote, logs the note, or pulls up the matching customer.

<Callout kind="note" collapsed="false">
  To keep a photo on the job itself — site photos, before-and-afters — add it to the job in Zoop. The chat connection reads what's in a picture; it doesn't store the picture.
</Callout>

## Sample conversations

Real exchanges you could have from your phone. (Names and links are examples.)

### Plan the day, then book a job

> **You** — What's on my plate today?
>
> **Zoop** — Three jobs today: pressure washing for Melton McGonagal at 9:00 AM, an under-sink leak for Melton at 11:00 AM, and a cabinet refinish for Peter Brown at 1:00 PM. Want the addresses?
>
> **You** — Book Edward Stone for a cabinet refinish Thursday at 9.
>
> **Zoop** — Booked it — Edward Stone, Thursday, June 18 at 9:00 AM. One less thing on your plate. Verify on Zoop: [https://app.zoop.example/your-business/jobs/8f21…](https://app.zoop.example/your-business/jobs/8f21…)

### Pull up what you need on site

> **You** — What's the gate code at Edward Stone's place?
>
> **Zoop** — Your note on that job says the gate code is 4821, around back.
>
> **You** — And his cell?
>
> **Zoop** — Edward Stone — (321) 784-5621.

### Draft an invoice from the truck

> **You** — Draft an invoice for the job I just finished at Edward Stone's — two hours of labor at $95 and a $45 part.
>
> **Zoop** — Drafted invoice for Edward Stone — two hours labor ($190.00) plus the part ($45.00), $235.00 before tax. Open it to review and send. Verify on Zoop: [https://app.zoop.example/your-business/invoices/1043…](https://app.zoop.example/your-business/invoices/1043…)

### Turn a photo into a quote

> **You** — \[sends a photo of a handwritten work order]
>
> **Zoop** — Read it as: repaint two bedrooms and the hallway, customer-supplied paint. Started a draft quote for Edward Stone with those lines so you can set prices and send. Verify on Zoop: [https://app.zoop.example/your-business/quotes/77b3…](https://app.zoop.example/your-business/quotes/77b3…)

## What it can reach is up to you

You're always in control of what a connected app can touch:

- When you first connect ChatGPT or Claude, it starts **read-only** — it can look things up, but not change anything.

- To let it book jobs, draft invoices, or add customers, an owner turns those permissions on — once — under **Settings → API & MCP**. You pick exactly which areas it can reach.

- Any owner can see everything that's connected, and cut it off in one tap, under **Connected apps**.

See [Control MCP & API access per member](/using-zoop/team) and [Settings](/using-zoop/settings) for the details.

<Callout kind="note" collapsed="false">
  Taking payment still happens in Zoop. Your assistant drafts the invoice; your customer pays through Zoop's secure payment link. See [Getting paid](/using-zoop/payments).
</Callout>
